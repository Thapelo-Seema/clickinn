About clickinn

Background

Clickinn is a platform that makes accommodation seeking much easier by providing a powerful search tool that is backed by a live accommodation-agent who is ready to assist with accommodation needs. This platform is a successor of the ClassFinder++ accommodation feature (which was launched in the beginning of 2017). Clickinn has not officially been launched yet but has started operating in January 2018.

The team behind the Clickinn platform is the same team that started ClassFinder++ with intention of providing an all-round student convenience product. The ClassFinder++ project currently revolves around; student accommodation, textbooks and graduation attire supplies. The team decided to single out the accommodation feature and make it a platform in its own right, which targets accommodation for the general market, including student accommodation and all non-student accommodation.

Mission

The mission of Clickinn is to connect the accommodation industry such that there will be no accommodation operating in isolation. In simple terms we want everyone (using clickinn) to be able to know about ALL(atleast 90%) the accommodations available in any area of interest.

Vision

We see ourselves as the Google Maps of accommodations, with more human-assisted services.

How does clickinn work ?

Clickinn lists partner accommodations which appear to visitors looking for accommodation. These accommodations are filtered accordingly when a user searches and user can browse through them. As soon as a user submits a search, accommodation agents are alerted and can respond with assistance to the user's search. The user can also directly chat with the advertiser of an accommodation of interest and arrange a booking. Accommodation seekers are currently not charged a cent for the whole service provided by the accommodation agents in assisting them to secure accommodation.

Areas covered

The platform is currently only operating in the Auckland Park area, the team anticipates to expand to other parts of Johannesburg within the next two months (February and March 2018) and to the Western Cape and Kwa Zulu Natal by June.
